                    Karma Generation
   The primary method for karma generation is by
making skill check rolls. When making a skill roll, if either
die rolled is a one, then the player has the choice of
either gaining one karma or one experience point for the
group. They must decide at that time and the decision is
final. If a skill check produces double ones, the character
receives 2 karma and one XP.
   Karma can also be produced by using some abilities
in the game. These abilities detail the method of
generation.

to one ratio, see the actions section. There are abilities
that alter this ratio.
                       Karma Usage
   Nudge Fate – When any die is rolled any player at the

die by one. This is not a +1 to the roll, it is changing the
value on the die. For rolls that explode, changing the
value causes the explosion as if the die had been rolled
with that value. In combat a player can nudge once per
round, remember this doesn’t have to be their turn. Out
of combat players can nudge until they do not have
karma remaining.

            Schrodinger Kits and Ammunition
    To reduce minutia many items are reduced into
Schrodinger kits that are assumed to have what you
would need. For example, a camping kit has a tent,
bedroll, blanket, short pieces of rope, stakes, etc. There
is no actual list of what is in a camping kit until you look
inside the kit. Additionally, things like ammo are not
tracked. If you have a quiver, every time you reach back
there will be an arrow. That level of resource tracking
takes away from the gameplay and the fun at the table
and does not typically add anything to the game.

Stat Block Description

    Breaking down the stat block of a creature is really straight forward. Ferocty is the number of actions the creature gets
per turn. Attack is the target number the characters must evade. Evasion is the target number characters use to hit the
creature. PV is the protection value representing armor either worn or natural. Initiative determines the creatures initiative
value. Remember in a group of creatures the entire group gets a value based on the creature with the highest initiative.
Morale is used to determine if the creature will flee and is also the target number for intimidation, deception, and
persuasion. Mobility is the amount of mobility resource the creature has. Any special attacks or abilities will then be listed
in special.
