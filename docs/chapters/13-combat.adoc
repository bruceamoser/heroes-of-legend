= Combat

Combat in Heroes of Legend is fast and cinematic. Every swing matters.

== Initiative
At combat start, each participant rolls 1d6 + Agility modifier. Highest goes first. Ties: higher Agility wins, then roll-off.

== The Combat Round
On your turn, take one *Standard Action* and one *Move Action*, plus any number of *Free Actions* and one *Reaction* per round.

[%header,cols="2,4"]
|===
| Action | Examples
| *Standard* | Attack, cast spell, use ability, dash, help, hide
| *Move* | Move up to speed, stand up, mount/dismount
| *Bonus* | Class features, quick items (conditional)
| *Reaction* | Opportunity attack, shield block (1/round)
| *Free* | Talk, drop item, gesture (unlimited)
|===

== Making an Attack
Attacks always hit. Roll 3d6 + modifiers to determine damage tier:
- *Weak:* Roll weapon's Weak damage (glancing blow)
- *Standard:* Roll weapon's Standard damage (solid hit)
- *Strong:* Roll weapon's Strong damage (devastating strike)
- *Critical:* Maximize Strong damage + critical bonus

== Damage Types
*Physical:* Slashing, Piercing, Bludgeoning
*Elemental:* Fire, Cold, Lightning, Acid, Poison
*Magical:* Force, Radiant, Necrotic, Psychic

== Conditions
[%header,cols="2,4,3"]
|===
| Condition | Effect | Ends
| *Blinded* | Attacks reduced one tier | Source duration
| *Charmed* | Cannot attack charmer | Save ends
| *Deafened* | Perception disadvantage | Source duration
| *Frightened* | Cannot approach source | Save ends
| *Grappled* | Speed 0 | Escape action
| *Incapacitated* | No actions | Source duration
| *Invisible* | Cannot be targeted directly | Attack/action ends
| *Paralyzed* | Incapacitated + auto-crit if hit | Save ends
| *Poisoned* | Disadvantage on attacks | Save ends
| *Prone* | Melee adv vs you, ranged disadv | Stand up (move)
| *Restrained* | Speed 0, attack disadv | Escape action
| *Stunned* | Incapacitated + cannot move | Save ends
| *Unconscious* | Incapacitated + prone + unaware | Healing or save
|===

== Surprise
When one side catches the other unaware, the ambushing side gains *initiative advantage*: they act first in the first round, regardless of initiative rolls. Surprised targets suffer a -2 penalty to their 3d6 roll on the first round — they're reacting, not acting.

To determine surprise, the ambushing side rolls Stealth opposed by the target's passive Insight (Knowledge score + 7). On Standard or Strong success, surprise is achieved.

> *DA Guidance:* Don't let the party surprise every encounter just because the rogue has high Stealth. Surprise requires genuine tactical advantage — darkness, distraction, or terrain. Walking up to a guard in plain sight and saying "I hide" isn't surprise.

== Grappling
To initiate a grapple, make a Brawn (Athletics) roll opposed by the target's Brawn (Athletics) or Agility (Acrobatics).

[%header,cols="1,4"]
|===
| Result | Effect
| *Weak* | You grab hold but don't control. Target is Grappled but can still act.
| *Standard* | Firm hold. Target is Grappled and Restrained.
| *Strong* | Complete control. Target is Grappled, Restrained, and you may move them at half speed.
|===

While grappling, you may use your Standard Action to:
- *Pin:* Force another opposed roll. Strong adds Incapacitated.
- *Throw:* End the grapple. Target is knocked Prone adjacent and takes Weak unarmed damage.
- *Choke:* Target begins suffocating (can hold breath for Fortitude + 2 rounds).

> *A grappling creature can still fight back.* The Grappled condition only prevents movement — a grappled spellcaster can still cast. The Restrained condition imposes disadvantage on attacks but doesn't prevent them.

== Two-Weapon Fighting
When wielding a weapon in each hand, you may attack with both as a single Standard Action. The off-hand weapon deals damage one tier lower (Strong→Standard, Standard→Weak, Weak→1 damage).

The off-hand weapon must have the *Light* property (Dagger, Shortsword, Handaxe) unless you have the *Dual Wielder* talent, which removes this restriction.

*Example:* Kael wields a longsword (primary) and a shortsword (off-hand). His 3d6 result is Standard for the primary (3 damage) and Weak for the off-hand (2 damage). Total: 5 damage.

== Non-Lethal Attacks
When you reduce a creature to 0 HP with a melee attack, you may declare the blow non-lethal. The creature falls Unconscious but stable at 0 HP instead of dying. Ranged attacks and spells cannot be made non-lethal unless the spell specifically allows it (e.g., *Sleep*).

> *Why this matters:* Prisoners have information. Killing the only goblin who knows the dungeon layout means you're wandering blind. Sometimes the most powerful thing you can do is leave someone alive.

== Morale
NPCs and monsters don't fight to the death by default. When a creature faces overwhelming odds, the DA may call for a Morale Check. The creature rolls 3d6 (no modifiers):

[%header,cols="1,4"]
|===
| Result | Behavior
| *Strong (13+)* | Stands firm. Gains +1 on its next attack.
| *Standard (7-12)* | Wavers but stays. Disadvantage on its next attack.
| *Weak (1-6)* | Flees or surrenders.
|===

A creature automatically checks morale when:
- Reduced below half HP for the first time
- Its leader is defeated
- Half its group has fallen
- The party demonstrates overwhelming force (DA discretion)

> *DA Guidance:* Use morale to keep combat from becoming a slog. When the goblins' leader falls and three of their friends are down, the remaining two should break and run — not fight to 0 HP while the party mops up. Morale makes combat feel dynamic and saves table time.

== Dying
At 0 HP, you fall unconscious. Each round, roll 3d6:
- *Strong (13+):* Stabilize at 1 HP
- *Standard (7-12):* Remain unconscious, stable
- *Weak (1-6):* Take 1 wound, continue dying
- *Fumble (3×1):* Death
- *Critical (3×6):* Regain consciousness at half HP

== Cover
*Half cover:* -1 to attacker's roll. *Three-quarters cover:* -3. *Full cover:* Cannot be targeted.
