= Advancement

Heroes grow through experience, gaining Development Points, dice types, and reputation.

== Gaining Levels
Levels are earned through adventure milestones. The DA awards levels when the party achieves significant goals. Expected pace: 1 level per 2-3 sessions.

== Development Points
At each level, gain 2-3 DP to spend on skills, talents, and abilities.

[%header,cols="1,1,2"]
|===
| Level | DP | Other Gains
| 1 | 3 | Class signature, starting dice
| 2 | 2 | —
| 3 | 2 | Progression die
| 4 | 2 | Attribute increase (+1, max +2)
| 5 | 3 | Class feature upgrade
| 6-20 | 2/level | Progression die every 3 levels, attribute every 4
|===

Total DP at level 20: 41. Total progression dice: 6. Total attribute increases: 5.

== Group Reputation
The party's renown grows with their deeds. Reputation affects NPC attitudes, quest availability, and title eligibility.

[%header,cols="1,3,2"]
|===
| Rep | Title | Effect
| 1-3 | *Unknown* | Standard prices, no recognition
| 4-6 | *Known* | 10% discount at friendly establishments
| 7-9 | *Renowned* | Free room and board, audiences with nobles
| 10-14 | *Heroic* | Followers, stronghold, legendary quests
| 15+ | *Legendary* | Immortalized in song and story
|===

== Reveling
Spend gold on legendary parties to boost reputation. 100 gp = +1 reputation for the session. Carousing may attract unwanted attention.

== Titles
Earned titles grant mechanical benefits. Examples: *Dragonslayer* (+1 damage vs dragons), *Knight of the Realm* (free lodging at any inn), *Archmage* (one free Adept spell chain).
