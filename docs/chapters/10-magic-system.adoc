= Magic System

In Heroes of Legend, magic always fires. No spell slots. No mana pool. When you cast, the magic happens -- the question is how powerful the effect will be.

== Always Fires
You do not roll to cast. You roll to determine effect strength. Every spell has Weak, Standard, and Strong outcomes.

== Spell Chains
Spells are organized into chains -- three tiers of increasing power:

[%header,cols="2,1,1,3"]
|===
| Chain | Novice | Adept | Master
| Fire Magic | Firebolt | Fireball | Volcanic Eruption
| Ice Magic | Ray of Frost | Ice Storm | Cone of Cold
| Lightning | Shocking Grasp | Lightning Bolt | Chain Lightning
| Healing | Cure Wounds | Prayer of Healing | Mass Heal
| Protection | Shield | Magic Circle | Globe of Invulnerability
| Charm | Charm Person | Suggestion | Dominate Person
| Illusion | Minor Illusion | Mirror Image | Greater Invisibility
| Necromancy | Chill Touch | Animate Dead | Finger of Death
| Divination | Detect Magic | Clairvoyance | True Seeing
| Transmutation | Enlarge/Reduce | Polymorph | Time Stop
|===

== Cantrips
Cantrips require no Discipline prerequisites. Every caster knows all cantrips in their tradition (arcane or divine).

== Arcane vs Divine
*Arcane* (Arcanist): Fire, Wind, Water, Earth, Energy spells. Manipulates natural forces.
*Divine* (Shepherd): Protection, Animal, Energy spells. Channels higher power. Focus on healing, warding, and radiant damage.

== Limitations
*Per-Encounter:* Powerful spells (Adept+) may only be used once per combat.
*Per-Session:* Master-tier spells are once per session.
*Concentration:* Some ongoing spells require concentration -- you cannot cast another concentration spell while one is active.
