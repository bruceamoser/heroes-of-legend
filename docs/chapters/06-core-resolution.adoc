= Core Resolution Mechanics

The heart of Heroes of Legend is a simple roll: *3d6 + modifiers*.

== The Core Roll

*3d6 + Attribute Modifier + Skill Bonus + Game Modifiers*

[%header,cols="1,1,3"]
|===
| Total | Tier | Meaning
| 1–6 | *Weak* | Partial success or complication
| 7–12 | *Standard* | Full success, expected outcome
| 13–18+ | *Strong* | Exceptional success, bonus effect
|===

With 3d6, results cluster around 10-11 — your skill matters more than chance.

[NOTE]
.Designer's Note — Why 3d6?
====
A single twenty-sided die is swingy. Every result is equally likely — the difference between a 1 and a 20 is pure luck. Three six-sided dice create a bell curve. Most rolls cluster around 10-11, which means your character's training — their attribute modifiers, their skill bonuses, their Disciplines — matters more than random chance. When you roll an 18, it *feels* special because it *is* special (about 1 in 216 rolls). The same goes for a 3. This system makes heroes feel competent at the things they've trained for, while leaving room for moments of extraordinary triumph and catastrophic failure.
====

== Critical Hits (Three 6s)

Automatic *Strong* result. Roll d6 for bonus:

[%header,cols="1,4"]
|===
| d6 | Bonus
| 1 | *Maximum Damage* — all dice roll max
| 2 | *Devastating Blow* — inflict stunned, prone, or disarmed
| 3 | *Moment of Glory* — bonus action or free move
| 4 | *Second Wind* — recover 2 HP or regain one ability
| 5 | *Inspired Strike* — ally gains advantage vs target
| 6 | *Narrative Flourish* — describe something epic
|===

== Fumbles (Three 1s)

Automatic failure. Roll d6 for complication:

[%header,cols="1,4"]
|===
| d6 | Complication
| 1 | *Weapon Trouble* — drop/jam, action to recover
| 2 | *Friendly Fire* — nearest ally takes Weak damage
| 3 | *Off Balance* — next attack vs you has advantage
| 4 | *Equipment Damage* — armor/weapon degrades one step
| 5 | *Spell Backfire* — take Weak damage from own magic
| 6 | *Narrative Complication* — scene worsens
|===

== Attacks Always Hit

No attack roll. Your 3d6 determines damage tier:

*Weak:* Roll weapon's Weak damage +
*Standard:* Roll weapon's Standard damage +
*Strong:* Roll weapon's Strong damage +

== Difficulty Modifiers

[%header,cols="1,3"]
|===
| +4 | Trivial task
| +2 | Easier than normal
| +0 | Standard challenge
| -2 | Harder than normal
| -4 | Very difficult
| -6 | Near-impossible
|===

== Worked Examples

*Kael (Brawn +2, Longsword +2) attacks a goblin.* Rolls 4+3+5=12, +2+2=16. *Strong!* Longsword Strong: 3d8+2 = 17 damage.

*Lyra (Knowledge +2) identifies a rune. Difficulty -2.* Rolls 2+4+4=10, +2-2=10. *Standard.* She knows it's Old Elvish — a warding glyph.

*Roric swings at a troll. 6, 6, 6!* *Critical!* Bonus roll: 4 — Second Wind (+2 HP). Strong damage: 26.
