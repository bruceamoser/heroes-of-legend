[#chapter-character-creation]
= Creating Your Hero

Building a hero takes about twenty minutes if you know what you want, or a
pleasant hour if you're still exploring. Either way, you'll end up with
someone worth playing. Here's how it works.

[NOTE]
====
*Two-Phase Creation*

Heroes of Legend uses a two-phase creation system. You build your hero's
background first (Level 0 -- who they were before adventure called), then
layer on their class training (Level 1 -- who they've become). Each phase
has its own pool of Development Points. You spend them all or lose them --
no banking. This isn't a spreadsheet. It's a story.
====

== Phase One: Background (Level 0)

This is who your hero was *before* they picked up a sword or opened a
spellbook. Every hero comes from somewhere.

=== Step 1: Concept

Who is your hero, in one sentence? Not their stats. Not their class. Their
*deal*. A disgraced knight seeking redemption. A fae-touched herbalist who
talks to bees. A dwarven smith who's never seen the sun and really, really
wants to. Write it down. Everything else flows from this.

=== Step 2: Attributes

Assign scores to your six attributes that total *exactly +3*:

*Brawn, Fortitude, Agility, Guile, Knowledge, Reason*

Each starts at +0. Max +2, min -2. Drop one into the negatives to fund a
strength elsewhere -- but the sum across all six must be 3. See
xref:chapters/03-attributes.adoc[Attributes] for the full breakdown.

=== Step 3: Ancestry

Choose your species. Each grants a Discipline and a trait:

[%header,cols="1,2,3"]
|===
| Ancestry | Discipline | Trait
| *Human* | Any one | *Versatile:* +1 DP at Level 0
| *Elf* | Archery or Blades | *Elven Grace:* once per session, reroll one die
| *Dwarf* | Axes or Armor | *Sturdy:* +2 maximum HP
| *Halfling* | Blades or Archery | *Lucky:* add one boon to any roll, once per session
|===

See xref:chapters/04-ancestries-cultures.adoc[Ancestries & Cultures] for
full details.

=== Step 4: Culture

Your upbringing within your ancestry. Each culture grants a +1 bonus to
one skill of your choice from its two options, plus either *two specific
Disciplines* that define its traditions, or *one free Discipline* for
adaptable cultures.

[%header,cols="1,2,3"]
|===
| Culture | Skill Bonus | Disciplines
3+^.^| *Human*
| Imperial | Persuasion or History | Any one (free)
| Nomadic | Survival or Animal Handling | Archery + Polearms
| Coastal | Athletics or Navigation | Polearms + Protection
3+^.^| *Elf*
| High Elf | Arcana or History | Energy + Blades
| Wood Elf | Stealth or Nature | Animal + Archery
| Twilight Elf | Deception or Insight | Water + Blades
3+^.^| *Dwarf*
| Mountain | Craft or Athletics | Armor + Axes
| Deep | Resilience or Lore | Axes + Protection
| Hill | History or Persuasion | Any one (free)
3+^.^| *Halfling*
| Riverfolk | Acrobatics or Sleight of Hand | Blades + Archery
| Burrower | Stealth or Survival | Protection + Blades
| Wanderer | Streetwise or Performance | Any one (free)
|===

See xref:chapters/04-ancestries-cultures.adoc[Ancestries & Cultures] for
full descriptions of each culture.

=== Step 5: Spend Background DP

Your hero didn't spring from the earth fully formed. They had a life
before this -- years of work, study, mistakes, and hard-won lessons. That
life is what Background DP represents.

You have *8 + Knowledge + Fortitude* Development Points to spend.

[NOTE]
====
*Why Knowledge and Fortitude?*

*Knowledge* measures education and capacity to learn. *Fortitude*
measures stamina -- how much training your body could endure before it
gave out. Together, they represent how much you absorbed from your
upbringing.

A farm kid with +2 Fortitude learned through years of grueling physical
labor. A temple scholar with +2 Knowledge learned through decades of
study. A character with both advantages is exceptional. A character with
neither had a harder start -- and hard starts make the best stories.
====

These points represent your *entire life* before Level 1. They do not
carry over into your class training. Spend every last one. Unspent
Background DP is potential you never realized -- lessons you could have
learned but didn't.

*What You Can Buy*

At Level 1, you are a *Novice*. You cannot buy Adept or Master tier
anything -- those unlock later, through experience.

Costs below are *X1 (Favored)* -- your class may multiply these. See
xref:chapters/05-classes.adoc#costs[Class Costs] for your class's
multipliers. Abilities are always 1/2/4 DP regardless of class.

[%header,cols="2,1,3"]
|===
| Purchase | Cost (X1) | Notes
| *Skill (Novice)* | 1 DP | +1 bonus. Adept (2 DP) at Level 3. Master (3 DP) at Level 7.
| *Ability (Novice)* | 1 DP | Martial, magical, or talent -- all abilities follow 1/2/4 DP. Adept at Level 3. Master at Level 7.
| *Discipline (first rank)* | 1 DP | First rank in a given type. No level gate.
| *Discipline (second rank)* | 2 DP | Deepening the same type. Stacks for ability prereqs.
| *Discipline (third rank)* | 4 DP | Mastery. Three ranks in Fire = serious flame.
|===

These represent everything your hero learned growing up. A farm kid might
take Athletics and Animal Handling. A temple acolyte might take Religion
and Medicine. A street thief might take Stealth and Sleight of Hand. Make
it make sense for your concept -- the DA will notice.

*General Disciplines*

All characters begin with *3 General Disciplines*. These are wildcards:
you assign each one to any Discipline type you choose, right now, for
free. They represent natural aptitude -- the things you were born with,
not the things you trained for.

Your ancestry and culture also grant Disciplines (see Steps 3 and 4).
Between General, ancestry, and culture, a starting hero typically has 5-7
Disciplines before spending a single DP. That's your foundation. Spend
your Background DP to build on it.

== Phase Two: Class (Level 1)

Now your hero finds their calling. This is where they become exceptional.

=== Step 6: Choose Your Class

[%header,cols="1,2,3"]
|===
| Class | Role | Vibe
| *Protector* | Tank, defender | "Stand behind me."
| *Blade* | Assassin, precision | "Strike once. Strike true."
| *Arcanist* | Arcane caster | "Fire answers to me."
| *Shepherd* | Divine guide | "The light guides my hand."
| *Intellect* | Scholar, strategist | "I've read about this."
| *Odd* | Chaos wildcard | "I have a trick for that."
| *Leader* | Commander | "Together, we are unstoppable."
| *Unbalanced* | High-risk power | "Power has a price."
|===

Record your Class Discipline (one rank) and signature ability.
See xref:chapters/05-classes.adoc[Classes] for full writeups,
including favored costs.

=== Step 7: Spend Class DP

You have *8 DP* for your class training. Again: spend every one. This is
where you buy skills and abilities -- combat techniques, spells, and
talents are all just abilities with different flavors. Your class lists
favored skills (X1) and favored Disciplines (X1); everything else costs
X2 or X3. Abilities always cost 1/2/4 DP -- their real gate is Discipline
prerequisites. Lean into what your class does best.

Adept-tier skills and abilities unlock at Level 3. Master-tier at Level 7.
At Level 1, everything you buy is Novice. That's fine. Novice means
you're trained. You'll deepen later.

=== Step 8: Equipment

Receive your class starting kit. Record weapons with their Weak, Standard,
and Strong damage values. Record armor with its Damage Reduction. See
xref:chapters/15-equipment.adoc[Equipment].

=== Step 9: Derived Stats

[%header,cols="1,3"]
|===
| Stat | Formula
| *Health Points* | 10 + Brawn (at Level 1)
| *HP per Level* | +Brawn (minimum 1) each level after first
| *Initiative* | Agility modifier
| *Movement* | 30 ft (reduced by heavy armor)
| *Carry Slots* | 10 + (Brawn × 5)
|===

=== Step 10: Backstory Touches

Roll or choose from the tables below. These are hooks for your DA -- and
for you.

.Motivations (d6)
[%header,cols="1,3"]
|===
| d6 | Why You Adventure
| 1 | *Glory:* You want songs sung about you.
| 2 | *Gold:* Wealth opens every door.
| 3 | *Justice:* Wrongs will be righted. By you.
| 4 | *Knowledge:* Secrets are the real treasure.
| 5 | *Protection:* Someone has to stand between the darkness and the innocent.
| 6 | *Redemption:* You're running from something. Or toward forgiveness.
|===

.Trinkets (d6)
[%header,cols="1,3"]
|===
| d6 | The Thing You Carry
| 1 | A key that doesn't fit any lock you've found. Yet.
| 2 | A locket with a portrait of someone you've never met.
| 3 | A coin that always lands on its edge.
| 4 | A letter, unopened. The seal is your family crest.
| 5 | A smooth stone that hums faintly at midnight.
| 6 | A child's drawing of you. You don't recognize the child.
|===

=== Step 11: Name

Choose a name fitting your ancestry and culture. Say it out loud. Does it
feel right? Good. Welcome to Heroes of Legend.

== Worked Example: Makeva Quickfoot

Let's build a character from scratch so you can see how the pieces fit
together.

*Step 1 -- Concept:* Charming sneak-thief with a heart of tarnished gold.
Grew up on the docks, talks her way into places she shouldn't be, always
has an exit strategy.

*Step 2 -- Attributes (total must be +3):* Agility +1 (nimble), Guile +1
(silver tongue), Brawn +1 (can throw a punch when the talking stops).
Fortitude +0, Knowledge +0, Reason +0. Sum = +3. ✓

*Step 3 -- Ancestry:* Halfling. Gains an Animal Discipline and the
*Lucky* trait (add one boon to any roll, once per session).

*Step 4 -- Culture:* Riverfolk. +1 bonus to Acrobatics (she grew up
scrambling across rigging and wet docks).

*Step 5 -- Background DP (8 + Knowledge 0 + Fortitude 0 = 8 DP):*
- Stealth Novice (1 DP) -- she's good at not being seen
- Deception Novice (1 DP) -- she's better at being believed
- Sleight of Hand Novice (1 DP) -- quick fingers, quicker pockets
- Perception Novice (1 DP) -- reading a room keeps you alive
- Streetwise Novice (1 DP) -- knows every fence in the harbor district
- Acrobatics Novice (1 DP) -- never met a rooftop she couldn't cross
- Water Discipline (1 DP) -- the river's in her blood
- Lucky talent, Novice (1 DP) -- some people are just born fortunate
Total: 8 DP. All spent. ✓
Disciplines: Animal(1), Water(1), General(3)

*Step 6 -- Class:* Odd. Gains 3 Disciplines from different categories (Archery,
Animal, Water). Signature: *Wildcard* -- once per session, roll a d6 table
for a random bonus.

*Step 7 -- Class DP (8 DP):*
- Archery Discipline (1 DP)
- Bow Fighting Novice (1 DP, favored)
- Stealth → Adept (2 DP)
- Deception → Adept (2 DP)
- Evasion Novice (1 DP)
- Dirty Fighting Novice (1 DP)
Total: 8 DP. All spent. ✓

*Step 8 -- Equipment:* Shortbow (W/S/S: 2/4/7), dagger (1/3/5), leather
armor (DR 2), thieves' tools, 20 arrows.

*Step 9 -- Derived Stats:* HP 10 (10 + Fort 0 + Know 0). Initiative +1.
Movement 30 ft. Carry 15 slots.

*Step 10 -- Backstory:* Motivation -- Gold (she's saving for something,
she won't say what). Trinket -- a silver coin that always lands on its edge.

*Step 11 -- Name:* Makeva Quickfoot. Her friends call her Mak. Her enemies
don't call her anything -- they never saw her coming.
