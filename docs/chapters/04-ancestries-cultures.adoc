= Ancestries & Cultures

Your hero comes from somewhere. They have a people, a homeland, a way of
life that shaped them before they ever picked up a sword. Ancestry is who
you're born as. Culture is how you were raised. Together, they're the
first chapter of your hero's story.

[NOTE]
====
*Ancestry Is Flavor, Not Fate*

A dwarf raised by elves is different from a dwarf raised in the mountain
holds. Your ancestry gives you a Discipline and a trait -- your biology,
essentially. Your culture gives you skills and perspective -- your
upbringing. You can mix any ancestry with any culture. The gruff mountain
dwarf raised by halfling riverfolk is not only allowed, it's *interesting*.
====

== The Four Ancestries

[%header,cols="1,2,3,3"]
|===
| Ancestry | Discipline | Trait | You're Probably...
| *Human* | Any one | *Versatile:* +1 DP at Level 0 | Ambitious, adaptable, everywhere
| *Elf* | Wind or Water | *Keen Senses:* +1 to all Perception checks | Ancient, graceful, a little haunted
| *Dwarf* | Earth or Fire | *Sturdy:* +2 maximum HP | Resilient, tradition-bound, impossible to move
| *Halfling* | Animal or any Guile | *Lucky:* reroll one 3d6 per session | Quick, cheerful, underestimated
|===

=== Human

*"We're not the strongest or the fastest. But we're still here, aren't we?"*

Humans are the most numerous and most varied of the ancestries. They build
empires and burn them down. They forget history and rediscover it. What
they lack in specialization they make up for in sheer adaptability -- a
human can be anything, and usually is.

Humans receive one Discipline of any type and one extra Development Point
at Level 0. They're the only ancestry with no fixed Discipline -- that
flexibility is the human superpower.

*Common human names:* Aldric, Brenna, Corwin, Della, Egon, Marta, Oswin, Petra, Rolf, Sigrid.

=== Elf

*"The trees remember your great-grandfather. So do we."*

Elves live long enough to see mountains erode. They carry the weight of
ancient pacts, forgotten wars, and songs written before human beings
discovered fire. This makes them patient, precise, and occasionally
insufferable at dinner parties.

Elves receive one Wind Discipline (speed, storms, precision) or Water
Discipline (ice, healing, adaptation). Their *Keen Senses* grant a
permanent +1 bonus to all Perception checks -- those pointed ears aren't
just decorative.

*Common elf names:* Aelindra, Caerwyn, Elowen, Faelan, Illyria, Lirael,
Orin, Sylvara, Theron, Vaelith.

=== Dwarf

*"Measure twice. Strike once. Bury deep."*

Dwarves are built like the mountains they call home -- solid, enduring,
and extremely difficult to move once they've made up their minds. They
invented metallurgy, perfected stonework, and hold grudges longer than
most civilizations last.

Dwarves receive one Earth Discipline (stability, endurance, stone) or
Fire Discipline (forge-craft, passion, destruction). Their *Sturdy*
constitution grants +2 maximum HP -- that's two more hits they can shrug
off with a grunt.

*Common dwarf names:* Baldrek, Darin, Gorma, Haldra, Kazrik, Morin,
Naldra, Rurik, Thaldrin, Ulfgar.

=== Halfling

*"Big things come in small packages. Also: breakfast, second breakfast, and elevenses."*

Halflings are small, quick, and possessed of an almost supernatural luck
that makes other ancestries quietly furious. They don't build empires.
They build communities, feast halls, and improbably comfortable burrows.
They're underestimated constantly, which suits them perfectly.

Halflings receive one Animal Discipline or any Guile-category Discipline
(Deception, Stealth, Streetwise). Their *Lucky* trait lets them reroll
one entire 3d6 roll per game session -- not just one die, the whole roll.
Use it when it matters.

*Common halfling names:* Bramble, Cora, Dabney, Eglantine, Finn, Lottie,
Makeva, Nettle, Pip, Rufus, Tansy.

== Cultures

Your culture is how you were raised. It's not just flavor — it's mechanical
identity. Each culture grants two things: a *+1 skill bonus* AND a unique
*benefit* that sets it apart from every other culture. Pick the one that
fits your story.

=== Human Cultures

[%header,cols="1,3,4,4"]
|===
| Culture | Skill Bonus | Benefit | You Grew Up...
| *Imperial* | Persuasion or History | +1 Development Point | In the heart of an empire. Politics, protocol, and the art of getting what you want with a smile. Education was not optional.
| *Nomadic* | Survival or Animal Handling | Bonus Discipline (Wind or Animal) | Following the herds, reading the stars. You can start a fire in a monsoon and never get lost. The open sky is your ceiling.
| *Coastal* | Athletics or Navigation | *Sea Legs* trait: ignore difficult terrain on ships, docks, and shorelines | Salt in your hair, sand between your toes. You swim before you walk, sail before you ride.
|===

=== Elf Cultures

[%header,cols="1,3,4,4"]
|===
| Culture | Skill Bonus | Benefit | You Grew Up...
| *High Elf* | Arcana or History | Bonus Discipline (Energy) | Among spires of crystal and libraries of starlight. Magic is breathing. History is yesterday.
| *Wood Elf* | Stealth or Nature | *Forest Walk* trait: ignore difficult terrain in forests and woodlands | In forests so ancient the trees have opinions. You move like wind through leaves.
| *Twilight Elf* | Deception or Insight | *Darkvision* trait: see in total darkness as if dim light, 60 ft range | In the places between light and shadow. Your people walked away from the sun long ago.
|===

=== Dwarf Cultures

[%header,cols="1,3,4,4"]
|===
| Culture | Skill Bonus | Benefit | You Grew Up...
| *Mountain* | Craft or Athletics | +2 maximum HP | In the high peaks, where the air is thin and the stone is stubborn. You can forge anything. You can climb anything. You can take a hit that would fold a horse.
| *Deep* | Resilience or Lore | *Darkvision* trait: see in total darkness as if dim light, 60 ft range | In halls carved miles beneath the surface. Darkness is comforting. Silence is companionable. You've never seen the sky and you're fine with that.
| *Hill* | History or Persuasion | +1 Development Point | In the foothills, trading with surface folk. You're the dwarf who explains dwarves to everyone else — and gets paid for it.
|===

=== Halfling Cultures

[%header,cols="1,3,4,4"]
|===
| Culture | Skill Bonus | Benefit | You Grew Up...
| *Riverfolk* | Acrobatics or Sleight of Hand | *Riverborn* trait: swim speed 30 ft, hold breath twice as long | On boats and barges, navigating rivers and trade routes. You can walk a gunwale in a storm and pick a pocket without breaking eye contact.
| *Burrower* | Stealth or Survival | *Burrow-sense* trait: detect movement through ground within 10 ft | In cozy tunnels beneath the hills, hidden from big folk and their big problems. You know every root, every mushroom, every hidden exit. The earth tells you who's coming.
| *Wanderer* | Streetwise or Performance | +1 Development Point | On the road, in the caravans, telling stories for supper. Home is wherever you hang your hat -- and you have a lot of hats. You've learned a little of everything.
|===
