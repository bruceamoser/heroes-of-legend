= Dungeon Architect's Guide

Advice for running Heroes of Legend at your table.

== Scene Management
Each scene should have a clear goal, conflict, and consequence. If nothing interesting can happen, skip to the next scene.

== Setting Difficulty
Modifiers guide the table. +4 for trivial, +0 for standard, -4 for very difficult. Don't call for rolls when the outcome isn't in doubt.

== Encounter Building
Balance encounters by comparing total party Disciplines to total enemy threat. A standard encounter matches the party's total. Hard encounters have 1.5×. Deadly encounters have 2×.

== Treasure Pacing
Award 1-2 magic items per level. Gold should feel meaningful -- parties should choose between gear, reveling, and reputation investments.

== NPC Creation
Quick NPC: assign one attribute at +1, one at -1, the rest at 0. Give them one skill at Adept (+2). That's enough for most interactions.

== Optional Rules
*Gritty Realism:* Short rest = 8 hours, long rest = 1 week. *Heroic Mode:* +1 DP per level. *Dice Crafting:* Allow players to combine 2 General Disciplines into 1 specific die at level 10+.

== Exploration & Travel
Overland travel uses a simple pace system. Each day, the party covers distance based on terrain and pace.

[%header,cols="2,2,2"]
|===
| Terrain | Normal Pace | Forced March
| Road/Grassland | 24 miles | 30 miles
| Forest/Hills | 18 miles | 24 miles
| Mountain/Swamp | 12 miles | 18 miles
| Desert/Tundra | 15 miles | 21 miles
|===

*Navigation:* Each day, one character makes a Survival (Knowledge or Survival skill) roll. Standard result: stay on course. Weak result: veer off course (1d6 extra hours). Strong result: find a shortcut (-2 hours).

*Random Encounters:* Roll 1d6 each day and night. On a 1, an encounter occurs. DA chooses or rolls from environment-specific tables.

*Camping:* A long rest requires 8 hours of rest including sleep. Characters on watch may make Perception checks at -2 (tired eyes).

> *DA Guidance:* Travel is a pacing tool. Use it to build atmosphere -- the oppressive silence of a swamp, the howling wind on a mountain pass. Skip travel entirely when the journey isn't the story. "After three days on the road, you arrive at the gates of Thornwall" is perfectly valid.

== Crafting
Characters with appropriate tools and skills can craft items during downtime.

[%header,cols="1,4"]
|===
| Result | Outcome
| *Weak* | Item is functional but flawed. -1 to its primary use.
| *Standard* | Item crafted as intended. Takes normal time.
| *Strong* | Masterwork quality. +1 bonus or half the normal time.
|===

*Time:* Crafting takes 1 day per 5 gp of the item's market value.
*Materials:* Cost half the item's market price in raw materials.
*Magic Items:* Require specific Disciplines, rare components, and a formula. Takes 1 week per tier (Novice/Adept/Master).

== Resource Management
Keeping track of supplies adds tension to exploration.

*Rations:* 1 per day per character. Without food, make a Fortitude (Endurance) roll each day. Standard result: function normally. Weak result: -1 to all rolls from fatigue.

*Ammunition:* A quiver holds 20 arrows or bolts. After combat, recover half on a Standard+ Survival roll. On a Weak result, recover only 1d4.

*Light:* Torches last 1 hour and illuminate 30 ft. Lanterns last 6 hours per flask of oil. Total darkness imposes -4 to any roll requiring sight.

*Optional -- Resource Dice:* For tables that prefer abstraction, use a Resource Die (d12→d10→d8→d6→d4→Depleted). Roll after each use; on a 1, the die steps down.

== Corruption
Proximity to dark magic, forbidden knowledge, and horrific events can corrupt a hero's spirit. The Unbalanced class embraces this -- but any character can be affected.

*Gaining Corruption:* The DA awards +1 Corruption for witnessing supernatural horrors, using forbidden artifacts, or making desperate bargains. The Unbalanced class gains Corruption as part of their class mechanic.

*Corruption Threshold:* 10 + Fortitude modifier. When a character reaches their threshold, they gain a permanent affliction (DA chooses based on the source -- nightmares, physical mutation, madness). At 15+, the character becomes unplayable.

*Recovery:* 1 Corruption fades per week of rest. Atonement quests, divine intervention, or rare magic can remove larger amounts.

> *Why this exists:* Corruption gives weight to dark choices. When the party debates whether to read the forbidden tome, the cost is real -- not abstract. It also gives the Unbalanced class a mechanical identity: they walk the edge willingly.

== Social Conflict (Extended)
For high-stakes negotiations, interrogations, or trials, use extended social conflict.

Each round, one side makes a social skill roll (Persuasion, Deception, or Intimidation) opposed by the target's Insight or relevant attribute. The first side to 3 successes wins the conflict.

[%header,cols="1,4"]
|===
| Result | Effect
| *Weak* | Your argument falters. Opponent gains 1 success.
| *Standard* | You score a point. Gain 1 success.
| *Strong* | Devastating point. Gain 2 successes.
| *Critical* | You break through entirely. Win the conflict immediately.
| *Fumble* | You offend or reveal weakness. Opponent gains 2 successes.
|===

*Stakes must be declared before the conflict begins.* "If I win, the guard lets us pass. If you win, we're arrested." This keeps social encounters from becoming endless persuasion checks.
