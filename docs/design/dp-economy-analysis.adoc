== Key Finding

All 6 characters spend **27–33 DP** on their focused builds (2 Master skills, 1 Master ability chain, 1-2 secondary chains, plus utility). This is 31–38% of budget 8 — and only 15–25% of budgets 12+.

At budget 8, characters have **55–61 unspent DP** by level 10 — room to double their breadth without running out.

At budget 12+, the surplus becomes absurd (99–149 DP). Characters would need to buy 3× more abilities than planned just to spend their points.

== Recommendation: 8 DP/Level

[quote]
____
8 DP per level (16 at level 1, 88 total over 10 levels) gives players enough to build a focused character with room for flavor picks, without drowning in unspent points.
____

- At 8 DP, a character can max 2 skills ($1 + 2 + 3 = 6$ DP each) and complete 2 ability chains ($1 + 2 + 4 = 7$ DP each) for ~26 DP — leaving 62 DP for secondary skills, utility abilities, and niche picks.
- At 12+ DP, the surplus is so large that DP stops being a meaningful constraint — every character ends up broadly competent, undermining specialization.
- **First-level double DP (16)** lets new characters buy 3-4 Novice skills and 2-3 Novice abilities, which is exactly what a level 1 adventurer should have.

== Remaining Questions for Bruce

1. **HP at levels 2–10:** Currently modeled as +Fortitude per level (min 1). This means low-Fortitude characters barely gain HP (Thalia goes from 12 to 12 — literally zero). Should there be a flat floor? (e.g., +1 HP minimum regardless of Fortitude, or +Fortitude + 1)

2. **DP on odd levels?** Do you gain DP at EVERY level or only even levels? (Model assumes every level.)

3. **Master ability cost:** 4 DP for a Master ability is double the Adept cost. Is this intentional? It means a full spell chain costs $1 + 2 + 4 = 7$ DP — more than maxing a skill ($1 + 2 + 3 = 6$ DP).

4. **General Disciplines:** Do the 3 starting General Disciplines become typed Disciplines at character creation, or do they remain "wildcard" slots that can be assigned later?

5. **Ancestry/background disciplines:** Do these stack with class disciplines for meeting prerequisites, or are they siloed?
