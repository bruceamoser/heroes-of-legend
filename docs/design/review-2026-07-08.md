#### What's Missing for a Fantasy Tome Aesthetic

To achieve the same level of visual identity that Neon Relic has, Heroes of Legend needs:

1. **A fantasy font** — NOT Liberation Serif. Options to explore:
   - **Crimson Text** (Google Fonts, open-source) — elegant oldstyle serif, bookish
   - **Gentium Book Basic** (SIL Open Font License) — designed for long-form reading
   - **Alegreya** (OFL) — literary serif with medieval character
   - **IM Fell** (OFL) — digitized from 17th-century typefaces, perfect for fantasy

2. **Decorative SVG elements** — chapter dividers with ornamental motifs (Celtic knots, heraldic flourishes, medieval border patterns), section stamps, illuminated drop-cap markers

3. **A title page with visual impact** — custom SVG with heraldic shield, decorative border, fantasy ornamentation

4. **In-world headers/footers** — chapter titles in a fantasy display font, decorative rules, flavor text appropriate to a fantasy tome

5. **A 4+ color fantasy palette** — warm parchment, dark brown ink, deep crimson accents, gold highlights for special callouts

6. **Styled admonitions** — illuminated-manuscript-inspired left borders, drop-shadow or ornamented note blocks

7. **Ornamental part dividers** — not just `<<<` but decorative rules with center ornaments between Parts

### 1.4 Terminology: "Dice" Means Two Things — A Major Confusion Source

The word "dice" is used for two completely different concepts throughout the rulebook:

| Usage | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **Physical dice** | The 3d6 you roll at the table | "Roll 3d6 + Attribute + Skill" |
| **Meta-currency** | A collection resource representing trained expertise | "Requires 2 Blade dice" |

A new player reading "you need 2 Blade dice to use a longsword" will naturally ask: "Do I roll them? Where do I put them? Are they physical dice I need to buy?" The confusion is systemic — it appears in every chapter.

**Resolution: Rename the meta-currency to "Disciplines."**

A Discipline represents one level of formal training in a domain. The term carries the weight of instruction, practice, and earned expertise — exactly what the mechanic models.

**Before → After (every instance across all chapters):**

| Old (confusing) | New (clear) |
|-----------------|-------------|
| "Fire dice" | "Fire Disciplines" |
| "Requires 2 Blade dice" | "Requires 2 Blade Disciplines" |
| "Dice type" | "Discipline" |
| "Dice prerequisite" | "Discipline prerequisite" |
| "Generic dice" | "General Disciplines" |
| "Starting dice" | "Starting Disciplines" |
| "Progression die" | "Progression Discipline" |
| "Dice type catalog" | "Discipline catalog" |
| "Chapter 08 — Dice Types" | "Chapter 08 — Disciplines" |
| "Dice Type System" | "Discipline System" |

**Prose examples:**

| Old | New |
|-----|-----|
| "A warrior with 3 Blade dice has spent years mastering swords." | "A warrior with 3 Blade Disciplines has spent years mastering swords." |
| "Characters collect dice types representing their mastery." | "Characters earn Disciplines representing their training." |
| "All characters start with 3 generic dice." | "All characters start with 3 General Disciplines." |

**Impact:** This rename touches every chapter, every table, every example, and the glossary. It's a mechanical find-and-replace but a *narrative* transformation — suddenly the system tells a story about training and mastery instead of sounding like you're collecting tokens.

**"General Disciplines"** replaces "Generic dice" — reads as "foundational training applicable anywhere" rather than "bland placeholder tokens."

### 1.3 Key Layout Problems

1. **No `[NOTE]` admonitions anywhere.** Neon Relic uses 60+ NOTE blocks to clarify edge cases, design philosophy, and DA guidance. HoL has none — every rule is presented as flat text with no nuance.

2. **No design philosophy explanations.** Neon Relic justifies every mechanical choice (why card initiative, why Corruption instead of Stress, why ties favor PCs). HoL states rules without explaining *why*.

3. **No recurring examples.** Neon Relic uses named characters (Petra Vasquez, Yusuf Demir) across multiple chapters to build continuity. HoL has two one-off examples that don't connect.

4. **No DA guidance sections.** Neon Relic chapters include explicit "when to roll," "how to adjudicate," and "common mistakes" sections. HoL chapters have none.

5. **Theme is functional but unadorned.** Liberation Serif is a workable font, but the theme lacks decorative elements (no SVG dividers, no chapter stamps, no ornamental breaks) that make a fantasy tome feel like a fantasy tome.

6. **Chapter 11 (Arcane Spells) and Chapter 12 (Divine Spells) are still placeholders.** The two largest chapters in the book (~300 spells) have zero content.

## Part 2 — Mechanical Analysis

### 2.1 Critical Issue: Damage Should Be Flat, Not Rolled

**User's stated intent:** "A long sword does 2/3/5 at W/S/S levels" — not 1d8/2d8/3d8.

**Impact of this design difference:**

| Approach | Weak | Standard | Strong | Expected Dmg (unmod) |
|----------|------|----------|--------|---------------------|
| **Dice (current)** | 1d8 (avg 4.5) | 2d8 (avg 9) | 3d8 (avg 13.5) | 9.75 |
| **Flat (intended)** | 2 | 3 | 5 | 3.43 |

**Lethality analysis:**
- HP range: 6 (FO -2) to 14 (FO +2). Typical HP: 10 (FO 0).
- With dice damage: A Standard hit (avg 9) wounds most characters. A Strong hit (avg 13.5) one-shots almost anyone. Combat is *extremely lethal* — first hit wins.
- With flat damage: A Strong hit deals 5 damage vs 10 HP — wounds but doesn't kill. Characters survive 2-3 Strong hits. Combat has tactical depth.

**Recommendation:** Rewrite ALL weapon tables, ALL spell damage values, and ALL combat examples to use flat damage values. The entire damage economy of the game changes.

### 2.2 3d6 Probability Analysis

| Modifier | Weak | Standard | Strong |
|----------|------|----------|--------|
| -2 | 25.9% | 64.8% | 9.3% |
| -1 | 16.2% | 67.6% | 16.2% |
| +0 | 9.3% | 64.8% | 25.9% |
| +1 | 4.6% | 57.9% | 37.5% |
| +2 | 1.9% | 48.1% | **50.0%** |
| +3 | 0.5% | 37.0% | **62.5%** |
| +4 | 0.0% | 25.9% | **74.1%** |

**Key findings:**

1. **At +2 modifier (Brawn +2, no skill), you get Strong hits 50% of the time.** A starting character with maxed Brawn is already hitting Strong half the time without any skill investment.

2. **At +4 modifier (Brawn +2, skill +2 Adept), you get Strong hits 74.1% of the time.** Weak results become impossible. The game becomes "roll to see if you get Strong or just Standard."

3. **The Standard tier (7-12) dominates at +0 — but only barely.** At 64.8%, most unskilled rolls are Standard — which is appropriate for baseline competency.

4. **Crit/Fumble rarity (0.46% each) is correct.** At 1/216, these feel special without being irrelevant.

**Problem:** The tier boundaries (1-6, 7-12, 13-18+) may be too generous. With even modest investment (+2 total), Strong becomes the default outcome. This flattens the tension between tiers. Consider: should tier boundaries shift with difficulty, or should modifiers be capped?

### 2.3 DP Economy Analysis

**Total DP at level 20: 41**

| What you can buy | DP Cost | How many at L20 |
|-----------------|---------|-----------------|
| Master one skill | 7 DP (N1+A2+M4) | ~5 skills at Master (35 DP) |
| Novice in all 22 skills | 22 DP (if all favored) | All skills at +1 |
| Talents | 1/2/4 DP | ~2-6 talents |
| Realistic build | Mixed | 3-4 Master skills + 2-3 talents |

**Problems:**

1. **41 DP over 20 levels feels tight.** A character can only master ~5 skills out of 22. That's narrow specialization at endgame.

2. **Cross-class DP costs (2-4×) make off-role skills prohibitively expensive.** A Protector wanting Stealth pays 3 DP just for Novice — 3× what an Odd pays. At that cost, cross-class skills are trap choices.

3. **No DP refund/respec rules.** What if a player regrets a purchase?

4. **Talents compete with skills for the same pool.** Every talent purchased is one less skill rank. This forces min-maxing.

**Questions:** Is 41 DP the right number? Should talents have a separate pool? Should cross-class costs be flatter (2× instead of 3-4×)?

### 2.4 Spell Chain Progression Analysis

**Fire Magic chain requirements:**
- Novice (Firebolt): 1 Fire die
- Adept (Fireball): 2 Fire + 1 Energy = 3 specific dice
- Master (Volcanic Eruption): 3 Fire + 2 Earth + 1 Energy = 6 specific dice

**Arcanist progression:**
- Starting: 4 elemental dice (e.g., 2 Fire, 1 Energy, 1 Wind)
- Progression dice: 6 total by level 18 (L3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18)
- Earliest Volcanic Eruption: Level 9 (need +1 Fire, +2 Earth from 3 progression dice)

**Problem:** Master-tier spells require 6 specific dice. A character who diversifies their dice across elements may never qualify for ANY Master spell. The system locks you into a specific elemental path very early.

**Questions:** Are 6 dice for Master too many? Should Generic dice be able to substitute at Adept tier (at higher DP cost)? Should there be "hybrid" spells that accept multiple element combinations?

### 2.5 Combat System Gaps

1. **No surprise rules.** How does ambush work in a system where attacks always hit?

2. **No mounted combat rules.** The equipment chapter mentions mounts but no mechanics.

3. **No underwater/special environment combat.**

4. **No morale or retreat rules for NPCs.** When do enemies flee?

5. **No non-lethal damage rules.** Can you knock someone out?

6. **No grappling beyond the Grappled condition.** How do you initiate a grapple? Opposed roll? What can you do while grappling?

7. **No two-weapon fighting rules.** Can a Blade dual-wield? How does that interact with always-hit?

8. **Initiative is d6 + Agility.** With Agility range -2 to +2, initiative range is -1 to 8. This is a very narrow spread — ties will be common.

9. **"Reaction: shield block" is mentioned but not defined.** What does shield block actually do mechanically?

**Missing categories essential for a core rulebook:**

- More beasts (giant spider, dire wolf, swarm)

## Part 4 — Questions for Bruce

### Core Mechanics
1. **Damage values:** Confirm — all weapons/spells use flat Weak/Standard/Strong values? Dagger: ?/?/? Longsword: 2/3/5? Firebolt: ?/?/? Fireball: ?/?/?  
2. **Attribute modifier to damage:** Should Brawn add to melee damage? If damage is flat (e.g., Longsword Strong = 5), does Brawn +2 make it 7? Or is the attribute already factored into the 3d6 roll determining which tier you get?
3. **Tier boundaries:** Are Weak 1-6, Standard 7-12, Strong 13-18+ the final numbers? At +4 modifier (Brawn +2, Adept skill +2), Strong hits 74% of the time — is that intentional?

### DP Economy
4. **DP per level:** Is 41 total DP at level 20 the right amount? Too tight? Too generous?  
5. **Separate pools?** Should skills and talents draw from the same DP pool, or should talents have their own progression track?  
6. **Cross-class costs:** Is 3-4× DP for off-role skills correct? A Protector paying 3 DP for Novice Stealth (vs Odd paying 1) — does this feel right?

### Disciplines (formerly "Dice Types")
7. **Master spell Discipline requirements:** Is 6 specific Disciplines for a Master spell (e.g., 3 Fire + 2 Earth + 1 Energy) too restrictive? Should General Disciplines substitute at Adept tier?  
8. **Total Disciplines at endgame:** A level 20 Arcanist has ~10 Disciplines total. Is that the right number?  
9. **Discipline count:** The current taxonomy has 14 types. Are there types missing (e.g., Shadow, Light, Mind, Time)?
10. **Terminology confirmed:** "Disciplines" is the final term? One last check before we rename it in every file.

### Magic
10. **"Once per encounter" for Adept spells:** What defines an encounter? If the party has two fights back-to-back with no rest, is that one encounter or two?  
11. **Cantrip power level:** Should cantrips scale with level (like D&D 5e), or stay static?  
12. **Divine vs Arcane distinction:** Is the split clear enough? Shepherds get Protection/Animal/Energy — do they get any unique mechanics beyond the spell list?

### Combat
13. **Always-hit with flat damage:** If a longsword Strong hit deals 5 damage, and HP is 10-14, combat takes 3+ hits to down someone. Is that the intended pace?  
14. **Armor as flat DR:** Plate armor reduces damage by 6. If a dagger Strong hit deals 3 damage, armor negates it entirely. Is that intentional? Should there be minimum damage (1)?  
15. **Dying mechanic:** The current 3d6-per-round death save — does this replace the "fun dying mechanic" from the original PDF where players mutter secrets and occasionally act?

### Scope & Priorities
16. **Spell catalog priority:** Should ~300 spells be written before the book is playable, or is a "starter grimoire" of 30-50 representative spells sufficient for early playtesting?  
17. **Bestiary size:** Minimum viable for core rules — 25 creatures? 50?  
18. **Subsystem priority:** Which missing subsystem is most critical for initial playtesting — exploration/crafting/social conflict/none?  
19. **Setting material:** The original PDF had backstory tables, motivations, trinkets, and personality traits. Should these be included in the core book, or reserved for a setting supplement?
