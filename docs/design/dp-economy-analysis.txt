HEROES OF LEGEND — DP ECONOMY ANALYSIS
Hand-built character progressions, Levels 0–5
============================================================

SYSTEM RULES (confirmed)
-----------------------
- 10 levels of play. Adept abilities unlock at Level 3, Master at Level 7.
- Disciplines: Serve as prerequisites. Purchased for 1 DP each.
- Skills: Rolled with 3d6. Novice +1 (1 DP), Adept +2 (2 DP), Master +3 (3 DP).
- Abilities: Special powers/spells. Novice 1 DP, Adept 2 DP, Master 4 DP.
- First level: DOUBLE DP (8×2 = 16 DP). Levels 2+: 8 DP each.
- HP: 10 + Fortitude + Knowledge at Level 1. +Fortitude per level (minimum 1).
- Attributes: 3 points to distribute, range -2 to +2.
- All characters start with 3 General Disciplines (can be assigned to anything).
- Class, Ancestry, and Background each grant additional Disciplines.

KEY INSIGHTS
------------
1. Wizards spend 1-2 more DP than martials by level 5 — the "spell tax."
   Each spell is a separate ability purchase. Martials get ongoing value
   from weapon skills; wizards pay per spell.

2. Discipline purchases are the real tension point. Pip needs 4 Discipline
   buys (2 Wind, 2 Water) to complete both chains — 4 DP that could have
   gone to skills or abilities. Meaningful choices.

3. Level 3 (Adept unlock) is the most expensive level for all characters.
   Characters come into their own at mid-levels — good pacing.

4. At 8 DP/level, no character is starved. At 12+ DP/level, Discipline
   costs become trivial and the tension disappears.

5. All characters have their core identity online by level 3-4. Levels 5-10
   are deepening and reaching toward Master (level 7+).


RECOMMENDATION
--------------
8 DP per level. Double at L1 = 16 DP. Levels 2+ = 8 DP each.
Total: 48 DP by level 5, 88 DP by level 10.
Creates meaningful tension between Disciplines, skills, and abilities
without starving any build concept.
