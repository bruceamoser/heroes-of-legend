# Design Ancestry

## When to Use
- Creating a new playable ancestry for chapter 04
- Designing ancestral traits and cultural backgrounds

## Procedure

### 1. RESEARCH
- Read `quarto-book/chapters/04-ancestries-cultures.qmd` for existing patterns.
- Read chapters 03 (attributes) and 02 (character creation).
- Check D&D 5e SRD for reference ancestry design.

### 2. DEFINE THE ANCESTRY
- **Physical description:** Size, distinctive features.
- **Cultural identity:** Values, traditions, typical roles.
- **Narrative hook:** Why choose this ancestry?

### 3. DESIGN TRAITS

#### Attribute Modifiers
Most ancestries: one +1 and one −1 (total +0). Some may have two +1s and one −1 (total +1). Keep total at +1 or 0.

#### Ancestral Traits (2–4)
- Always-on traits: Darkvision, resistances, natural weapons.
- Situational bonuses: +1 to specific checks in certain conditions.
- Unique abilities: Something only this ancestry can do.

#### Culture Options (2–3)
Each culture provides a small mechanical benefit and flavor description.

### 4. WRITE THE ENTRY
```markdown
## [Ancestry Name]

[1–2 paragraphs of physical and cultural description.]

**Attribute Modifiers:** +1 [Attr], −1 [Attr]
**Size:** [Small/Medium]
**Speed:** [Base movement]

### Ancestral Traits
- **[Trait]:** [Description and effect.]
- **[Trait]:** [Description and effect.]

### Cultures
#### [Culture Name]
[1 paragraph of flavor.]
**Cultural Benefit:** [Mechanical benefit.]
```

### 5. REVIEW
- Attribute modifiers sum to +1 or 0.
- Traits balanced against existing ancestries.
- Each culture has a distinct identity.
- Matches generic fantasy, heroic adventure tone.
